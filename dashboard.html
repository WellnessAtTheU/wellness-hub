What the item codes mean. These are the four items on the NCHA-III campus-climate battery. Each is a self-report scale answered by students. Q1 is a general-health question (response scale: Excellent → Poor). R2B, R2C, R2D are perception-of-institution items on a 6-point agreement scale (Strongly Disagree → Strongly Agree). Values below are top-3 (Good+) for Q1 and top-2 (Agree+Strongly Agree) for R2B/R2C/R2D.

Observations

  • R2B is the drag. "Health is a priority" moved slightly the wrong direction. All other items gained.
  • Open discussion gained most. R2C +2.3 pp is the largest single-item gain.
  • U of U ≠ Consortium. Every R2 item remains 5–7 pp below peer institutions.
  • Q1 (general health) exceeds peers. U of U students perceive personal health favorably even as they rate institutional climate lower.

Interpretation

  • Anxiety rose 5 pp. The largest shift across HMS cycles is a clinically meaningful increase in GAD-7 positive screens. This moves anxiety from a 1-in-4 issue to 1-in-3.
  • Depression also rose. PHQ-9 positive screens rose 2 pp — smaller than anxiety but directionally concerning.
  • Flourishing edged up. 35% → 37% flourishing suggests the top of the distribution is holding while the bottom is worsening.
  • Loneliness stable. Three UCLA-3 items are essentially flat, suggesting social isolation plateaued at pandemic-era levels.
